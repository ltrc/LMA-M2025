Course Information

Course Description

Large Language Models (LLMs) are transforming how tasks are automated and personalized across domains. As LLMs evolve into interactive agents, they are set to drive the next major breakthrough in AI. This course introduces the foundations of LLMs, key capabilities for task automation, and essential infrastructures for building LLM agents. We will explore applications such as generation, tool augmentation, web automation, healthcare, and scientific research, while also addressing current limitations and future directions.

Students will learn the theoretical foundations of transformer architectures, attention mechanisms, and modern training techniques including pre-training, fine-tuning, and reinforcement learning from human feedback. The course also covers AI agents, multi-agent systems, tool use, reasoning capabilities, and real-world applications. Students will implement key algorithms and build practical systems throughout the course.

Prerequisites

Students should have completed at least one graduate-level introductory course in AI/ML, such as Introduction to NLP, Statistical Methods in AI, Advanced NLP, or Information Retrieval and Extraction.

Mini Project

The aim of this mini-project is to build a small autoregressive language model (LM) for multiple languages from scratch.
This is an individual project where you will handle the entire workflow from data collection to training and fine-tuning the model.

Project Timeline

Phase 1: Data Collection & Preprocessing

Collect corpus for three languages: English (50% of total tokens), Mother tongue (30â€“40% of total tokens), and an Indian language (10â€“20% of total tokens).
Collect atleast 3 billion tokens in total and perform data preprocessing including cleaning, deduplication, and segmentation.

Deliverables: Scripts and token statistics.

Phase 2: Tokenization

Train a tokenizer such as BPE, SentencePiece etc. and ensure that the vocabulary supports all three languages adequately.

Deliverables: Tokenizer files and evaluation of token coverage.

Mid-evaluation

Due: 28th August 2025

Deliverables: Phase 1 and Phase 2 deliverables along with report explaining the work done and action plan for Phases 3-5.

Phase 3: Pre-training

Use the given model type with a parameter size between 100M-150M and train an autoregressive LM using the processed corpus and a suitable framework such as PyTorch, Tensorflow, etc.
You may use free-tier compute platforms such as Kaggle, Google Colab, etc. with checkpointing to avoid losing progress due to resource limits.

Deliverables: Pretrained model checkpoints and training logs

Phase 4: Fine-tuning on Reasoning Tasks

Fine-tune the pretrained model for the 2 reasoning tasks assigned to you using chat-style templates.Do not make the model memorize the tasks- this will be checked during evaluation.

Deliverables: Fine-tuned model and scripts.

Phase 5: Evaluation and Analysis

Due: 15th September 2025

Evaluate the pre-trained and fine-tuned models using metrics such as perplexity, accuracy etc. , perform error analysis and discuss your observations.

Deliverables: Phase 3 and Phase 4 deliverables along with a detailed report containing your approach, methodology, experiment details, results and discussion of the results.
